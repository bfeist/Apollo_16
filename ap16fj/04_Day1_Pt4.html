[Apollo 16 has completed the Trans Lunar Injection burn(TLI) of the S-IVB third stage of the Saturn V booster. The S-IVB nowholds only 2.75 tonnes of the 108.45 tonnes of propellant that it waslaunched with, the balance having been consumed to place the stage andthe attached Apollo spacecraft on the long, three day ballistic coastto the Moon. The 40 metre long, 65 tonne "stack" of booster andspacecraft is travelling at 36,360 kilometres per hour, but is slowingdown rapidly since it is still subject to approximately one quarter ofEarth's surface gravity. It will continue to slow down until the Moon'sgravitational pull exceeds that of the Earth, in about two days time.]
